What is hi?

hi is leveraging blockchain technology to build services that are community powered. Members of hi are the key stakeholders of this ecosystem and the business is committed to maximize membership value - not profits. Our first service is the world’s first universal cross-ecosystem mobile payment and financial services platform.

  • Transfer money to your friends, even those who do not have a hi account yet.

  • Get rewarded for using hi.

What is a channel?

To be universally accessible, hi does not require the download of a new mobile application. Instead, you connect with your account through the world’s most popular social media and communication platforms. How do I refer new users?

A new user can be invited via a referrers link (e.g. www.hi.com/nickname) to a hi channel in order to be registered as a referral of yours. Alternatively, any CashHash you’ve generated that is deposited into or money you send to a new account, it is automatically registered as a referral of yours. You are entitled to 50% of new customer bonuses received by your referrals, including all downstream rewards they receive from referring others.

What personal details do I need to provide to sign up?

Initially, nothing other than your phone number for communicating through a hi channel. In order to deposit or withdraw money, you will need must pass a basic know-your-customer (KYC) verification check. Details about KYC will be provided soon.

Can I access one account through multiple channels?

Yes. hi recognizes your phone number from different channels. We will allow you to combine multiple accounts into one. KYC will ensure that there is one account for each member.

What is the difference between fiat/traditional currencies and digital/crypto currencies?

Fiat/Traditional currency is “legal tender” backed by a “central government.” It can take the form of physical dollars (e.g. paper Federal Reserve notes), or it can be represented electronically, such as with bank credit. A government controls the supply. Common examples include the US dollar, the Euro, and the Japanese Yen.

Digital/Crypto currency is not “legal tender” and it is not backed by a central government or bank (it is decentralized and global). An algorithm or smart contract controls the supply. Common examples include Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Litecoin.
